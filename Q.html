Summary of Findings¶

Total Sales Leaders: Older Families/Budget, Young Singles-Couples/Mainstream, and Retirees/Mainstream generate the highest total sales — but this is driven by large customer bases, not high spend per customer.

Highest total sales: Older Families/Budget, Young Singles-Couples/Mainstream, Retirees/Mainstream — driven mainly by having large customer bases, not necessarily high per-customer value.

Highest value customers: Older and Young Families spend the most per customer (~$32–35) and buy the most units (~8.6–9.3 per customer) — nearly double that of Young Singles/Couples (~$15–19, ~4.3–4.6 units).

Pack size: Not a meaningful differentiator between segments — preference is fairly consistent across all groups (~173–178g average).

Brand preference: A meaningful differentiator — Families (both Older and Young) consistently favor value/everyday brands: Woolworths, Red Rock Deli, Sunbites, CCs, Natural Chip Co, Cheetos, Burger Rings — and consistently avoid premium/mainstream leaders like Kettle, Pringles, Doritos, Tostitos.

Recommendation¶

Target marketing and promotions toward Families (Older and Young) as the highest-value segment on a per-customer basis, specifically promoting value-oriented, everyday snack brands (Woolworths, Red Rock Deli, Sunbites, CCs) rather than premium lines, since that's where their actual purchasing behavior already sits. Meanwhile, Young Singles/Couples remain a high-volume segment worth retaining, but with lower expected return per customer, so may warrant lower-cost engagement (e.g. digital/loyalty app promotions) rather than the same investment level as Families.
